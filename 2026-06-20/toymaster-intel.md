## At a glance

**🟢 Strengths**

Market-leading position for UK independent toy retailers (est. 1977; 40+ years). TIMS is a genuine competitive advantage - automated consolidated invoicing saves member retailers significant admin time vs managing 150+ supplier relationships independently. Member recruitment growing (7 new Garden Centre branches in Jan 2025 alone; specialist wargaming retailer also joined). Managing Director Yogi Parmar is publicly bullish on indie sector despite macro headwinds. Year-end rebate structure aligns Toymaster incentives with member success.

**🔴 Weaknesses & pain points**

Very thin margin (~0.6% on £48.8m revenue) means cost control at HQ is critical. Budget 2024 pressures (NIC hikes, living wage increases, business rates relief cuts) are squeezing member retailers - creating downstream payment risk for Toymaster's own supplier obligations. Accounts paper-filed and scanned (no digital financial transparency). Small HQ team means payment operations are likely manually intensive. Two long-serving NED board members departing in 2026 (Ciaran Fitzpatrick April 2026, Julian Hartfield July 2026) - governance transition moment.

**🟡 Payment posture**

TIMS is the core infrastructure: it consolidates 150+ supplier invoices into one monthly statement per member and Toymaster settles with each supplier on the member's behalf. This is a classic principal-model buying group payment flow. The gap between member payment collection and supplier disbursement is the float window Toymaster carries. Given member cost pressures in 2025 (Budget headwinds), the risk of member payment delays to Toymaster - and therefore Toymaster's own supplier obligations - is elevated.

**🟡 PRQ / PayFac signal**

Green (Medium confidence). TIMS documentation is explicit: 'Toymaster pay all Suppliers on the Member's behalf' and 'one single monthly payment to Toymaster'. Member-owned structure, no marketplace language, no PayFac signals. SIC 46190 (agent) but the principal payment model is clear from TIMS terms. Full PRQ was run at discovery stage (verdict confirmed Green).

## PRQ preliminary findings

**Central contracting party - not a marketplace or PayFac**

TIMS terms explicitly state: 'Toymaster pay all Suppliers on the Member's behalf' and 'one single monthly payment to Toymaster'. Toymaster is the legal counterparty to both suppliers (paying them) and members (invoicing them). This is a principal B2B settlement model, not a marketplace or payment facilitation model.

**SIC 46190 (Agent) - mitigated by payment model**

Companies House lists SIC 46190 ('Agents involved in the sale of a variety of goods'). However, the TIMS payment model is that of a principal, not agent: Toymaster pays suppliers and invoices members in its own name. The SIC code likely reflects the buying group's commercial function (negotiating on behalf of members) rather than the payment structure. Full PRQ (already run) returned Green, validating this assessment.

**Member-owned buying group structure**

Toymaster board is composed of member retailer representatives as NEDs (Christopher Blatcher, Roger Dyson, John Testori, with new NEDs from member businesses). Year-end rebate based on group turnover is a classic mutuality feature. No investor ownership, no external principal.
