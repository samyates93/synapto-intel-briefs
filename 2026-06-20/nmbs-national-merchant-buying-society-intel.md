## At a glance

**🟢 Strengths**

Market-leading buying society position (est. 1963; 60+ years). £2.22bn buying power significantly outscales competitors (Fortis ~£1bn; NBG smaller). Payment Pledge is a genuine competitive differentiator - suppliers prefer NMBS because they get a debt guarantee. OnePlace/OpenECX digital ordering platform is ahead of peer groups. New CEO with strong operational credentials from Travis Perkins and Headlam. Member-owned mutual structure aligns incentives.

**🔴 Weaknesses & pain points**

Payment operations remain largely manual at a scale of £2.22bn - nine-person Purchase Ledger team and nine-person Allocations & Credit Control team indicate significant manual overhead. Accounts held with FCA Mutuals Register only (limited external financial transparency). CEO succession risk following 47-year tenure of predecessor. Head of Finance recently filled (vacancy existed). No Trustpilot or consumer-facing review presence (pure B2B - not a weakness per se, but limits external signal quality).

**🟡 Payment posture**

NMBS is the central contracting party: it buys from 500+ suppliers and sells on to 1,300 members. The Payment Pledge makes NMBS the guarantor of all supplier invoices. Member payment to NMBS is a single consolidated monthly invoice (across all supplier purchases), reducing member complexity. However, the settlement layer between the consolidated member payment and individual supplier disbursements appears to run through a large manual Purchase Ledger team - a clear automation gap at £2.22bn scale.

**🟡 PRQ / PayFac signal**

Green (Medium confidence). Co-operative buying group structure: NMBS is the principal, not an agent or marketplace facilitator. No marketplace or PayFac language. Not FCA regulated as a payment institution. Full PRQ has already been run (discovery verdict confirmed). No further structural risk signals identified.

## PRQ preliminary findings

**Central contracting party - not a marketplace or PayFac**

NMBS self-describes as a 'buying society'. It acts as the central buyer from 500+ suppliers and the sole creditor of 1,300 members. The Payment Pledge confirms NMBS pays suppliers as principal, not as agent. Discovery row PRQ rationale: 'co-op self-description; non-FCA'. No marketplace, facilitator, or PayFac language observed anywhere on site.

**Registered Society (FCA Mutuals Register) - not FCA regulated for payments**

IP16406R is a Registered Society under the Co-operative and Community Benefit Societies Act. This is a governance registration (mutual structure), not a payment institution registration. NMBS processes payments as principal (buyer/seller), not as a regulated payment service provider. No FCA FIRM number found.

**Single consolidated invoice model eliminates PayFac risk**

Members make a single monthly payment to NMBS (not to individual suppliers). NMBS then disburses to 500+ suppliers. This is a principal-to-principal B2B settlement model, not a split-payment or marketplace disbursement.
