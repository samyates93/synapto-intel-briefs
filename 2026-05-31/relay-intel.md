**🟢 Strengths**

Strong B2B retailer client base (ASOS, THG, Vinted, TikTok, Temu). Proprietary AI routing and computer vision POD technology. Experienced founding team from Stuart. Plural (Taavet Hinrikus) and Project A on the board. Flexipay next-day courier pay as a product differentiator.

High burn rate (GBP 11.8m FY25) against GBP 8m inferred revenue. No CFO on register - finance absorbed into 60-person team. Glassdoor courier reviews flag pay accuracy issues. Geographic coverage still London and Manchester only. Trade creditors up 108% YoY as fleet scales.

**🟡 Payment posture**

B2B retailers pay Relay on net-30 to net-60 invoice terms. Relay pays self-employed couriers next-day via Flexipay (Copilot app withdrawal). Float gap is structural and widening with scale. HSBC Innovation Bank venture debt facility (Jan 2026) shows Relay is already managing working capital externally.

## PRQ preliminary findings

**MoR-compatible language in privacy clause**

Privacy clause states 'self-employed couriers ... on our behalf' and 'fulfil our contracts with the retailers' - Relay appears to contract with retailers as principal and sub-contracts delivery to couriers. No formal payment T&Cs published beyond S7 boilerplate. Full PRQ required before commercial engagement.

**Flexipay = direct payout from Relay to couriers**

Relay's Copilot app offers 'Withdraw earnings next day with Flexipay'. Couriers paid directly by Relay, not by retailers. Relay absorbs all payment risk and timing - consistent with MoR or ISV pathway.

**Employment status discrepancy between 2023 seed round press and current language**

Seed-round TechCrunch article (Nov 2023) stated 'Local couriers receive sick pay, holiday pay, and other benefits'. Current discovery row and privacy clause cite 'self-employed couriers'. Classification may have evolved. Requires fresh T&C review.
