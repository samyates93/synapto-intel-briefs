## PRQ preliminary findings

**Seller of record on all consumer transactions**

Bagista sets prices algorithmically (per discovery source). T&Cs place all contractual liability with Bagista. Consumer buys from Bagista, not from consignor. Both outright-purchased and consigned items are sold under the same merchant banner.

**Consignor payout is a disbursement, not a split**

T&Cs state: payment issued no sooner than T+7 from buyer receipt. 70% of sale price to consignor per discovery. If buyer returns during window, payout cancelled and consignment resumes. This is a downstream disbursement, not a real-time split or regulated client money arrangement.

**Sub-floor price guarantee - note**

Discovery source flags 7,000 lifetime pieces sub-floor risk, suggesting Bagista may guarantee consignors a minimum floor price regardless of sale. This is a commercial liability (provision risk) but does not change merchant-of-record status. Watch for confirmation in any full accounts that show provisions.

**No FCA registration; SIC 47722**

SIC code 47722 (retail sale of leather goods). No payment institution or EMI licence found. Not a regulated marketplace. Medium confidence only because T&Cs are thin and no acquirer/PSP is named.

## Outreach angles

### Primary, CEO / Founder

**Margin: Knightsbridge luxury ticket sizes make default card rates an expensive default**

Hi Param - at your average transaction size, you are almost certainly on a blended rate from your current payment provider. A Birkin at £15,000 or a Chanel at £4,000 is a very different processing profile to a £40 online purchase, and blended pricing does not reflect that.

Synapto prices on an interchange-plus basis - you pay actual interchange plus a fixed margin, which for high-value luxury transactions saves 0.5-0.8 percentage points over blended Stripe rates. On £3m+ GMV, that is £15,000-£24,000 per year that currently goes to your payment provider and could stay in Bagista.

With expansion boutiques planned for the next few years, now is the moment to lock in proper processing economics before the volume grows further. Happy to run the numbers on what you are currently paying.

**Expansion timing: sort the payment infrastructure before opening the next boutique**

Hi Param - congratulations on the record Knightsbridge sales. The next boutique is an exciting milestone - and it is also the moment when a payment setup that works fine at one location starts to show its limitations across two or three.

The companies that get this right upgrade their processing rates, consignor payout automation, and reconciliation before they expand - not after. Synapto handles all three in one platform.

Would be good to share how two other luxury resale businesses prepared their payment infrastructure ahead of their expansion. Happy to schedule 20 minutes.

**Operational: consignor payout automation before scale demands it**
