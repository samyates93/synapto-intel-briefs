# Designer Exchange

**PRQ: Green** (High) · ICP 1 · **Margin wedge** · uk.designerexchange.com

> UK's largest preloved luxury retailer buying designer items outright and reselling across six stores (UK + Madrid) and online.

---

## At a glance

**🟢 Strengths**

UK's largest preloved luxury retailer by review volume: 4.3/5 on Trustpilot with approximately 6,000 reviews and 88% five-star rating. Multi-channel presence across six stores (Manchester, Birmingham, Newcastle, Cheshire Oaks, Knightsbridge, Madrid) plus active online. Strong double-lock authentication combining AI and human expert review. Established brand since 2013 with consistent growth in store count.

**🔴 Weaknesses & pain points**

Growing accumulated losses at parent entity level (-£3.6m Dec 2023, up from -£2.5m Dec 2022, approximately £1m loss per year). Very low cash at parent level (£197k). No identifiable CFO or Finance Director in Companies House filings. Payment back-office appears manual - Admin Assistant role on the careers page explicitly lists 'payment processing, banking reconciliation, and system updates' as core duties.

**🟡 Payment posture**

Outright cash-buy retail model: Designer Exchange is the merchant on both sides - it buys items from sellers outright and sells to consumers directly. No marketplace payout structure, no Stripe Connect. Consumer purchases accepted via Visa, Mastercard, Maestro, Amex, Apple Pay, PayPal, and Klarna. Multiple premium card brands on high luxury ticket sizes (handbags, watches, jewellery in the £200-£5,000 range) makes blended card cost a material line.

**🟡 PRQ / PayFac signal**

PRQ: Green (full verdict confirmed at discovery). T&C 10.3.8 explicitly states title passes to Designer Exchange on purchase. No Stripe Connect. Not a marketplace - Designer Exchange is the merchant and buyer throughout. Full PRQ completed at discovery stage.
