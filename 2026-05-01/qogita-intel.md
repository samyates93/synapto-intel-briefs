## Payment flow

**SME Retailers and ecommerce buyers** (10,000+ buyers; bank transfer, wallet, Mondu BNPL) → **Qogita + Synapto** → **Wholesale sellers** (500+ authorised brand distributors, 28 countries)

- Pays in: B2B
- Payouts: Per-order settlement to 500+ wholesale sellers across Europe and UK
- Beneficiary type: Brand-authorised wholesale distributors
- Float window: For BNPL orders (30-90 day terms), Mondu pays Qogita on fulfilment and collects from buyer - Qogita float is limited for BNPL portion. For non-BNPL orders (bank transfer, wallet), the float between order fulfilment (paying sellers) and buyer payment receipt depends on payment method timing. At $1.5B+ GMV, even a few days of float is a material cash management exercise.

## PRQ preliminary findings

**Title and MoR confirmed by T&Cs**

Buyer T&Cs: 'full ownership of the goods will remain with Qogita until [payment]'. Qogita holds title to goods until buyer payment. This is a clean principal/seller MoR structure, not an agent or facilitator.

**Invoices in own name**

T&Cs: 'Buyer agrees to pay Qogita for the Pay Later Amount'; invoices issued by Qogita. The contractual and billing relationship is directly between Qogita and the buyer.

**BNPL partner is Mondu - not a PayFac architecture**

Qogita's BNPL offering uses Mondu (Berlin B2B BNPL fintech) as the financing partner. Mondu takes on buyer credit risk. This is a financing arrangement behind the MoR structure, not a PayFac/Stripe Connect model. Qogita remains the invoicing party. Source: Qogita help centre (help.qogita.com/how-can-i-apply-for-bnpl).
