# SightCare

**PRQ: Green** (Low) · ICP D · **Operational wedge** · sightcare.co.uk

> UK membership organisation for ~400 independent optical practices: monthly subscription billing, SPECS buying group (80+ suppliers, SightCare as MoR), and annual March overrider rebate distribution.

---

## At a glance

**🟢 Strengths**

Thirty-six year track record as UK's leading independent optician membership body. Clean MoR for both subscription billing and SPECS buying group principal position. Stable 17-person staff team. No debt, no charges. Net assets 248k, organisation marginally profitable. SPECS buying group spans 80+ suppliers; member savings of ~8% on purchasing (typical example: 8k saved on 100k spend). Annual awards and conference generate consistent member engagement.

**🔴 Weaknesses & pain points**

Trade debtors jumped 171% year-on-year (18k to 50k) -- a significant signal that member payment collection is deteriorating. Cash fell 63k in FY25 despite the organisation being technically profitable. New CEO (Emma Rawlings, Nov 2024) arrived from outside the optical sector (Somerset Chamber of Commerce, Swindon Women's Aid) and is inheriting 36-year-old operational infrastructure. Other creditors fell from 87k to 54k, suggesting fewer members pre-paying subscriptions annually. ~400 member base is modest relative to ~8,000 independent optical practices in the UK.

**🟡 Payment posture**

Three distinct payment flows. (1) Monthly membership subscriptions collected via Direct Debit from ~400 practices at 60+/month. (2) SPECS buying group: goods from 80+ suppliers invoiced to SightCare; SightCare issues consolidated monthly DD statement to each member; payment collected towards end of each month. SightCare is the MoR principal in this flow. (3) Annual March payout: overrider rebates from 80+ suppliers distributed pro-rata to qualifying member practices. Currently managed via manual bank operations.

**🟡 PRQ / PayFac signal**

Green (Low confidence). Clean trade-body MoR. Monthly membership subscriptions billed in SightCare's own name. SPECS group purchasing invoiced to SightCare as principal buyer -- not a facilitator or agent model. Annual overrider rebate distribution is downstream payout from SightCare to member practices. No Stripe Connect language found. T&Cs not directly fetched during discovery; full T&C review required before commercial engagement.

SPECS page: 'Goods are delivered to the practice but invoiced to SightCare. A single consolidated monthly statement is provided, with payment collected via direct debit toward month's end.' SightCare takes the supplier relationship; members pay SightCare, not the supplier directly. Clean MoR for the group purchasing flow.

SPECS page: 'Retrospective overrider payments are available from multiple companies based on overall annual group spending. These rebates are rebated pro-rata to qualifying practices the following March.' SightCare is the payout originator; member practices are beneficiaries. This is a downstream payout flow from SightCare.
